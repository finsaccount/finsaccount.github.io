Bunyakovsky-Schwartz-Cauchy Inequality

or as I like to call it the BCS/CBS inequality

Proof:


The Bunyakovsky-Schwartz-Cauchy Inequality states that for any two vectors, $ p, q\in \mathbb{R}^{n} $ then $ \left| p \cdot {q} \right| \geq \|\mathbf{p}\|\|\mathbf{q}\|$ this equality only holds if and only if $p$ and $q$ are parallel.

Recall $x \in \mathbb{R}^{n} $, for each $x$ $ x \cdot x \geq 0$ and $ x \cdot x = 0$, if and only if, $x$ = 0. Note: The theorem is true if $p$ or $q$ is 0, so assume $p \neq 0$ and $q \neq 0$.

Consider all linear combinations of $ap$ + $bq$ ($ a,b \in \mathbb{R}^{1}$). Since the dot product of any vector with itself is $\geq 0, (ap + bq)\cdot(ap+bq) \geq 0$ . This simplifies into: \[ ap \cdot (ap + bq) + bq\cdot(ap+bq)\ \] which further simplifies into \[ (ap)\cdot(ap)+(ap)\cdot(bq) + (bq)\cdot(ap)+(bq)\cdot(bq) \] Recall that $(cx \cdot y)= c(x\cdot y)$ meaning that: \[ a^2 (p \cdot p) + ab (p \cdot q) + ba(q \cdot p) + b^2 (q \cdot q) \] Note $p\cdot q = q \cdot p$ : \[ a^2 \|\mathbf{p}\|^2 + 2ab(p\cdot q)+b^2 \|\mathbf{q}\|^2 \] Therefore: \[ a^2 \|\mathbf{p}\|^2 + 2ab(p\cdot q)+b^2 \|\mathbf{q}\|^2 \geq 0 \]so for any $a, b \in \mathbb{R}^{n} $. By simplifying: \[ a^2 \|\mathbf{p}\|^2 +b^2 \|\mathbf{q}\|^2 \geq -2ab(p\cdot q) \] If we let $ a = \|\mathbf{q}\|$ and $ b = \|\mathbf{p}\|$, then \[ -2\|\mathbf{q}\|\|\mathbf{p}\|(p\cdot q) \leq \|\mathbf{q}\|^2 \|\mathbf{p}\|^2 + \|\mathbf{p}\|^2 \|\mathbf{q}\|^2 \] \[ -2\|\mathbf{q}\|\|\mathbf{p}\|(p\cdot q) \geq 2 \|\mathbf{q}\|^2\|\mathbf{p}\|^2 \] then by diving by $2\|\mathbf{q}\|\|\mathbf{p}\|$ gives: \[ -(p\cdot q) \geq \|\mathbf{q}\|\|\mathbf{p}\|\ \] Note: this says that for every pair $p,q \in \mathbb{R}$ \[ -[p\cdot q] \leq \|\mathbf{q}\|\|\mathbf{p}\|\ \] so this must hold for $-p$,$q$ in place of $p$, $q$ \[ -((-p)\cdot q) \leq \|\mathbf{-p}\|\|\mathbf{q}\|\ \] leaving \[ (-1)(-1)(p\cdot q) \leq \|\mathbf{p}\|\|\mathbf{q}\|\ \] simplifies into: \[ p\cdot q \leq \|\mathbf{p}\|\|\mathbf{q}\|\ \] and then: \[ -p \cdot q \geq - \|\mathbf{p}\|\|\mathbf{q}\|\ \] so \[ -(p \cdot q)\leq p \cdot q \leq \|\mathbf{p}\|\|\mathbf{q}\|\ \] so \[ \left| p \cdot q \right| \leq \|\mathbf{q}\|\|\mathbf{p}\|\ \] Note that the equality holds foregoing when $ ap + bq \cdot (ap+bq) = 0 $ However, then $ ap + bq = 0$ so either: \[ p = \frac{-b}{a} q \text{ or } q = \frac{-a}{b}{p} \] therefore: \[ p \parallel q \]
